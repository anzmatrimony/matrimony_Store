Barnier Public School Swimming Carnival 2017

The Barnier Public School swimming carnival is for all students in years 3-6. Children in year 2, turning 8 in 2017, are invited to attend if they can swim 50m and are competing in an event (but are not able to attend as a spectator).

Where: Riverstone Poll – Market St & W Garfield Rd, Riverstone
When: Wednesday 22 nd February 2017 (Buses leave the school at 8:50am and will return by 2:45pm).
Cost: The cost of this event is $12.00. This includes bus fare and pool entry.Please return payment with the Semester 1 invoice payment no later than Friday 17th February.
Food/Drinks: Please provide students withy plenty of food and water for the day. There is canteen available at the pool for students to purchase recess and lunch. Alternatively, students can purchase an excursion pack from the school canteen. Please see flyer for cut off dates.
Time Approximate Timetabling of Events
9:30 – 10:00 100m Freestyle (open age event)
10:00 - 12:00 50m Freestyle Heats
12:00 – 1:15 50m Breaststroke, 50m Backstroke, 50m Butterfly
1:15 – 1:45 50m Freestyle Heats
1:45 – 2:00 200m individual Medley (IM)

Things to note:

  • Finals will be held for the 50m freestyle events. All other events will be judged on times from the heats.
  • Freestyle is run in age groups. The 100m freestyle is an open aged event. The 200m IM is raced as junior (8, 9 & 10 years) and Senior (11, 12, & 13 years). All other strokes are run in Junior (8, 9 & 10 years), 11 years and Senior (12 & 13 years) categories.
  • There will be NO novelty events due to issues with supervision, timing and Work Health and Safety requirements. All students in 3-6 are welcome to attend the carnival as competitors or spectators. If you choose for child not attend the carnival, there will be adequate supervision and teaching programs at school for the students.
  • Parents are more than welcome to attend although must pay a spectator entry fee to Riverstone pool and make their own transport arrangements to and from the venue.
  • It has been a request from pool staff that no children at all are to be in the small pool. This is ensur that we have enough lifeguards on duty in the appropriate areas for our carnival
  • At the conclusion of the carnival if you choose to sign out your child they must be taken from the pool immediately (expectation from Riverstone pool). Any child who has been signed out will be marked as ‘partial absence – unjustified’.
